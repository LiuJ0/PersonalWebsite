About

I aim to solve complex problems arising in the real world by designing rigorous methods with provable guarantees, and I am particularly interested in problems rooted in deep mathematical theory. These interests span:

  • Mathematical statistics: empirical process theory, non-parametric methods, and assumption-light methods.
  • Applied analysis: interacting particle systems, optimal transport, and partial differential equations.
  • Applications to genetics: network analysis, disease gene discovery, and large-scale hypothesis testing.

I am fortunate to have been mentored by Dr. Jinchuan Xing (Rutgers Genetics), Dr. Min Xu (Rutgers Statistics), and Dr. Matthew Rosenzweig (CMU Math).
